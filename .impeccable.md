## Design Context

### Users
Small business owners and founders — primarily in Singapore but expanding worldwide. Non-technical. Time-poor. Already spending money on social media agencies and freelancers. Anxious about AI search but unsure where to start. They make fast decisions and respond to clarity and proof, not jargon.

### Brand Personality
**Bold. Urgent. Provocative.**

Scantiv challenges the reader. It creates discomfort and FOMO. It speaks like a peer who already knows the answer and is waiting for you to catch up — not like a consultant trying to earn your trust slowly. The voice is direct, a little wry, and respects the reader's intelligence while being unafraid to say uncomfortable things.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme:** Light mode. Warm cream/beige neutrals (not cold white). Green accent (`#0D7C6B`).
- **Typography:** Suisse Int'l — Swiss precision, not friendly-rounded.
- **Motion:** Intentional fade-ups and spring-physics on interactive elements. Subtle, not playful.
- **Anti-references:** Generic SaaS dashboards, purple-to-blue gradients, glowing AI aesthetics, glassmorphism, template-feeling card grids.
- **References:** Editorial, refined, confident. Feels like a premium report, not a startup landing page.

### Design Principles
1. **One idea per sentence.** No compound sentences that bury the point.
2. **Discomfort before solution.** The problem has to land before the product is introduced.
3. **Specificity builds trust.** "24 hours", "SGD 99", "0–100 score" — concrete numbers beat vague claims.
4. **Never undercut urgency.** Every word of copy should make the reader feel that not knowing is a risk.
5. **The reader is busy, not stupid.** Write for someone who skims but rewards clarity with attention.
